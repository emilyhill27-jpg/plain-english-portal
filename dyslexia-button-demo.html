SCIENCE — Year 5/6 — Living World

Name: _____________   Date: _____________

Read the passage below and answer the questions that follow.

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants manufacture their own food using sunlight energy. During photosynthesis, plants absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere through tiny pores called stomata, located primarily on the underside of leaves. Simultaneously, water is transported from the roots through the xylem vessels to the leaves. Using chlorophyll, the green pigment found in chloroplasts, plants convert these raw materials into glucose and oxygen. The glucose is utilised as an energy source for growth and reproduction, while the oxygen is released into the atmosphere as a by-product.
  1. Define the term 'photosynthesis' in your own words. (2 marks)
  2. Identify the THREE raw materials required for photosynthesis to occur. (3 marks)
  3. Explain the function of stomata and describe where they are predominantly located. (3 marks)
  4. What role does chlorophyll play in the process of photosynthesis? (2 marks)
  5. Describe TWO products of photosynthesis and explain what happens to each. (4 marks)
