Additional validation rules for MSD/Benefits documents.

These checks apply on top of the general validator rules. They reflect the specific risks when explaining Work and Income forms, letters, and notices.

MSD PRESERVATION CHECKS:

P1. Dates and deadlines — Has every date, deadline, and timeframe from the original been preserved exactly? MSD documents often contain appointment dates, review dates, stand-down periods, and response deadlines. Missing any of these can cause real harm.

P2. Dollar amounts and payment details — Has every dollar amount, payment frequency (weekly, fortnightly), abatement rate, and recoverable/non-recoverable distinction been preserved exactly? Do not round, approximate, or convert between payment frequencies.

P3. Obligations — Has every obligation been clearly stated? MSD documents describe things the person must do to keep their benefit. If the original says "you must" or "you are required to", the output must preserve that requirement clearly.

P4. Consequences and sanctions — Has every consequence been preserved? If the original describes what happens when an obligation is not met (benefit reduction, suspension, overpayment recovery), the output must state this clearly. Do not soften, minimise, or bury consequences.

P5. Conditions and eligibility criteria — Has every condition or eligibility requirement been preserved? If the original says "you qualify if" or "you must meet these criteria", every criterion must appear in the output. Do not summarise multiple conditions into a single shorter statement.

P6. Review and appeal rights — If the original mentions the right to ask for a review, request a benefit review, or dispute a decision, has this been preserved? These rights are important and must not be omitted.

P7. Required actions and documents — Has every required action (attend appointment, provide documents, report income changes) and every required document (medical certificate, payslips, ID) been listed?

MSD ADDITION CHECKS:

A1. No eligibility advice — Does the output avoid telling the person whether they are eligible or ineligible? The output should explain what the document says about eligibility, not make a determination.

A2. No benefit calculations — Does the output avoid calculating or estimating benefit amounts beyond what the original document states?

A3. No application advice — Does the output avoid recommending whether to apply, appeal, or take any specific action? It should explain what the options are, not recommend one.

A4. No medical interpretation — If health conditions are mentioned, does the output avoid adding medical commentary or interpretation?

A5. No softening of obligations — Does the output preserve the mandatory nature of obligations? "You must" should not become "you might want to" or "it would be a good idea to".

MSD CLARITY CHECKS:

C1. Document purpose — Does the output clearly state what the document is and why the person received it?

C2. Next steps — Does the output clearly explain what the person needs to do next, if anything?

C3. Key information upfront — Are dates, amounts, and required actions surfaced near the top of the output, not buried in the middle?

C4. MSD terminology — Are MSD-specific terms (obligation, sanction, abatement, stand-down, recoverable grant) explained in plain English the first time they appear?
