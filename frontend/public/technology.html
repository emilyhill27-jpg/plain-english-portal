Text to speech — built in, zero cost

Every simplified document can be read aloud using your browser's built-in speech engine — no API, no per-use cost, no data leaving the device. Users can click any word to start playback from that point, adjust speed (0.5x to 1.25x), and follow along with word-by-word highlighting. Designed for low-literacy readers, people with dyslexia, and anyone who processes language better through listening.

Personalised reading level

Choose the reading level before simplification — from Year 3 primary school through to adult general English and professional modes. The AI targets that exact level, not a one-size average. A WINZ letter simplified for a 9-year-old reads very differently to one simplified for a 14-year-old. Both are right, for different people.

WCAG and neuroinclusive reading toolbar

Every reader is different. The built-in toolbar lets users adjust font, text size, line spacing, and background tint — without touching any settings page. Changes apply instantly to the simplified document. Designed to WCAG 2.2 AA and COGA cognitive accessibility standards throughout.

Neuroinclusive design

Every adjustment. Every reader.

Plainly is built to the W3C's WCAG 2.2 and COGA standards — not as an afterthought, but from the ground up. That means every feature in the reading environment is designed to support people with dyslexia, ADHD, autism, low literacy, and cognitive differences.

🔊

Text to speech

The entire simplified document can be read aloud. Users can click any word to start playback from that point — and control the reading speed. Designed for low literacy readers and people who process language better through listening.

🎨

Visual comfort controls

Background tints (cream, blue, lilac, grey) reduce visual stress for readers with Irlen syndrome or photosensitivity. Lexend — a typeface designed to improve reading fluency — is the default. Users can switch to Open Sans at any time.

📐

Structure and spacing

COGA Objective 3 guides how simplified content is structured: short sentences, active voice, plain vocabulary, no idioms. Line spacing and text size controls reduce crowding for readers who experience visual processing difficulties.
