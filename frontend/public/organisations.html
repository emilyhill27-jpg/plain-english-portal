Plain Language Act 2022 · S.4 Purpose
To improve the effectiveness and accountability of public service agencies by requiring documents to use language that is appropriate to the intended audience, and clear, concise, and well-organised.
— Plain Language Act 2022, New Zealand Parliament · Royal assent 21 October 2022
⚠️

In April 2025, the government introduced a bill to repeal this Act — calling it "a waste of time and money." The repeal removes the legal requirement for public agencies to communicate clearly. It does not remove the 26% of New Zealanders reading at literacy Level 1 or below. It does not remove the 851,000 people living with a disability. The need is still here. The law is leaving. Plainly stays.
