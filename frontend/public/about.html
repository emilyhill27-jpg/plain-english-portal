About Plainly.

Plainly explains important documents in plain English — without changing what they mean. Built in Aotearoa New Zealand, from lived experience of neurodivergence.

Why Plainly exists

Documents should not be a barrier.

Plainly started because of a real problem. Forms from government agencies, letters from schools, tenancy agreements, insurance policies — important documents that people need to act on, written in language many people cannot easily follow.

For neurodivergent people, low-literacy readers, and anyone unfamiliar with official language, these documents create confusion, missed deadlines, and avoidable phone calls. The information is there — it is just not accessible.

Plainly was built to close that gap. Not by replacing the original document, but by explaining it clearly alongside it.

What Plainly does

Plain-English explanations of real documents.

Plainly takes approved document types and explains them section by section in plain New Zealand English. The original document stays visible — the explanation sits alongside it.

  • Explains forms, letters, agreements, and notices in plain English
  • Keeps every date, amount, condition, deadline, and obligation intact
  • Flags important details — what to gather, what to watch for, what to do next
  • Offers audio playback, adjustable reading level, and reading support tools
  • Processes documents in real time — nothing is stored after the session ends
How we design

Neuroinclusive from the ground up.

Plainly is not a standard document tool with accessibility added on afterwards. Every design decision is made with neurodivergent and low-literacy users in mind first.

  • Calm, low-stimulation interface — no pop-ups, no dark patterns, no ads
  • Reader support toolbar — users control text size, font, spacing, and background tint
  • Text-to-speech with word-by-word highlighting and speed control
  • Designed to WCAG 2.2 AA and COGA cognitive accessibility standards
  • Plain language throughout — no jargon, no assumptions about prior knowledge

Trust and boundaries

What Plainly does — and does not do.

Plainly is a reading support tool. It helps people understand documents. It does not replace professional advice.

Plainly will:

  • Explain what a document says in plain English
  • Preserve every detail from the original — dates, amounts, conditions, obligations
  • Tell the user clearly when something in the original is unclear or incomplete
  • Process documents in real time and discard them when the session ends

Plainly will not:

  • Give legal, medical, financial, or eligibility advice
  • Store documents, create user accounts, or track individuals
  • Guess, speculate, or fill in missing information
  • Summarise away important details to make text shorter
