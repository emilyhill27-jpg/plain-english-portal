Privacy

Last updated: May 2026

What we collect

When a document is processed through Plainly, we generate a plain-English explanation. We do not store documents after processing. Once the session ends, the document content is gone.

What we don't do

  • We don't store your uploaded documents or images
  • We don't create user accounts or track individuals
  • We don't sell or share your data with third parties
  • We don't use your documents to train AI models

AI processing

Plainly uses Anthropic's Claude AI to process documents. Your document content is sent to Anthropic's API for processing and is not retained by Anthropic after the response is generated. Anthropic's data retention policy confirms that API inputs are not used for model training.

For organisations

When an organisation uses Plainly under an organisation licence, we may collect anonymised usage metadata: document count, page count, tool used, and timestamp. No document content is stored. This data helps organisations understand how Plainly is being used.

New Zealand Privacy Act 2020

Plainly is built in Aotearoa New Zealand and operates in accordance with the Privacy Act 2020. Because we don't collect or store personal information from documents, the privacy risks are minimal. If you have questions, contact us at hello@tryplainly.co.nz.
