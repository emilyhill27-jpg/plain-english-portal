# MSD Style Notes

## Tone
- Respectful and supportive. Never patronising.
- Use "you" for the client, "Work and Income" or "we" for the agency.
- Do not use "beneficiary" — say "person receiving a benefit" or just "you".

## Common confusions
- "Obligation" vs "requirement" — MSD uses both. Standardise to "obligation" and explain it.
- "Review" can mean two things: a scheduled check-in, or a formal dispute. Make clear which one.
- "Appointment" can be in-person, phone, or video. Always clarify if the document does.

## Numbers
- Always include the exact dollar amount, not "approximately" or "around".
- Always include the exact date, not "soon" or "shortly".
- If a payment is weekly, say so. If fortnightly, say so. Do not convert between them.

## Sensitive areas
- Income changes: explain that not reporting income changes can lead to overpayments that must be repaid.
- Sanctions: explain what happens clearly but without adding fear. State the fact, not the emotion.
- Medical certificates: explain what the document needs but do not give medical advice.
