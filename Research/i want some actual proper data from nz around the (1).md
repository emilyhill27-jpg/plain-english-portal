# i want some actual proper data from nz around the cost of contact centre calls, drop offs, complex forms

Honest answer: there is **no single, publicly available NZ-specific dataset** that neatly compiles cost-per-call, form drop-off rates, and complex form abandonment in one place. However, here's the best verified data I could find, broken into NZ-sourced and credible international benchmarks applicable to NZ.

***

## NZ-Specific Data Points

**NZ call centre industry size:** The NZ call centre operation market was worth **NZ\$191 million in 2026**, with 75 businesses, but has been *declining at a CAGR of 6.2% since 2020* — largely due to digital channel shift.[^1]

**NZ government digital savings:** The NZ Government expects to save **NZ\$3.9 billion over 5 years** through centralised digital investment and procurement, signalling the scale of cost currently tied up in manual/assisted service channels.[^2]

**Digital.govt.nz's own FormBuilder:** The platform explicitly exists because complex forms are a documented problem — agencies were taking weeks to build forms that caused abandonment and unnecessary call-backs.[^3]

***

## Cost Per Call (No NZ-Specific Published Figure)

There is no publicly released NZ government figure for cost-per-call, but international benchmarks widely cited in the sector put it at:

- **\$3–\$6 per call** (average, general contact centre)[^4]
- **\$7+ per call** for complex or assisted transactions[^4]

For NZ government agencies like IRD or MSD handling high-complexity calls (benefits, tax, immigration), the per-call cost is almost certainly at the higher end given NZ labour rates.

***

## Form Abandonment Data

These are global figures but are the most credible available and widely used in NZ UX/service design contexts:

- **34% of people** who start an online form fail to complete it[^5]
- Local government forms take on average **8.5 minutes to complete** — abandoners typically drop off after **3 minutes**[^5]
- **67% of users** will abandon a form entirely if any usability issue is encountered[^5]
- **60% of job seekers** abandon applications due to length or complexity[^5]
- Phone number fields cause **37% abandonment** unless made optional[^5]
- Forms with dropdown fields have the **highest abandonment rates** of any field type[^5]

***

## What This Means for Plainly

The gap is real and documented — complex language is a known driver of both form abandonment and unnecessary contact centre calls. The best NZ-specific sources for publishable data would be:

- **Stats NZ / Digital.govt.nz** — publish annual digital inclusion and service stats
- **Te Kawa Mataaho (Public Service Commission)** — their State of the Public Service report tracks channel costs[^6]
- **IRD Annual Reports** — occasionally publish contact volume and digital uptake data
- **MSD / Workandincome.govt.nz** — known to have significant call centre load from confusing forms

If you want hard numbers for a pitch deck or business case, I'd recommend an OIA request to IRD or MSD asking for cost-per-call and digital transaction cost data — that would give you citable NZ government figures.
