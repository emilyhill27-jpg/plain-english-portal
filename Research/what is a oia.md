# what is a oia

An **OIA (Official Information Act)** is New Zealand's law that gives you the right to request any information held by government agencies.[^1]

Passed in 1982, it covers citizens, permanent residents, and anyone in NZ — including businesses. You can request reports, emails, internal memos, data, policies, decision-making guidelines — basically anything a government agency holds. The agency **must respond within 20 working days** and can only withhold information if there's a specific legal reason to do so.[^2][^3]

## How It's Useful for Plainly

For your use case, you could send an OIA to **IRD, MSD, or MBIE** asking specifically for:

- Cost per inbound phone call (or contact centre cost per transaction)
- Volume of calls driven by form or document confusion
- Digital vs. assisted channel transaction cost comparisons

You submit it by email — for example, MSD's is at **oiarequest@msd.govt.nz**. It's free, straightforward, and the data you get back is citable and NZ-specific, which would be far more powerful in a pitch deck than international benchmarks.

A good resource for writing your request is [oia.nz](https://oia.nz).[^4]
